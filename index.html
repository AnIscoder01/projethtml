Tunisia (in Arabic: تونس), formally known as the Tunisian Republic (in Arabic: الجمهورية التونسية), is an Arabic-speaking and predominantly Muslim sovereign state in North Africa since 1956. Bordered to the north and east by the Mediterranean Sea (1,566 km of coastline), to the west by Algeria with a common border of 965 km, and to the southeast by Libya with a 459 km border, its capital Tunis is located in the northeast of the country, at the bottom of the gulf of the same name. Over 30% of the territory is occupied by the Sahara Desert, with the remaining consisting of mountainous regions and fertile plains.
